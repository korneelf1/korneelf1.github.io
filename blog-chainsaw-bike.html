The Chainsaw Bike

2012 Archive

When I was around 11, I wanted to build a motorized bike. The idea was to use a broken chainsaw engine from our shed and mount it on a bicycle.

Initial Sketches

I started with some sketches of what I had in mind. The first one was inspired by motorcross bikes I'd seen.

Early concept sketch with enclosed wheels and other features I thought would be cool.

After realizing that was a bit ambitious, I made a more realistic sketch based on what I could actually build with the parts I had.

A more realistic design using scrap parts and the chainsaw engine.

The Build

The final version was simpler than either sketch. I mounted the chainsaw engine on a mountain bike and connected it to the rear wheel using a friction drive system.

  • Base: Mountain bike
  • Engine: Small 2-stroke chainsaw motor
  • Drive: Friction against rear tire

Result

It worked, sort of. The bike moved under its own power, which was the goal.

Testing it out.

The chainsaw eventually went back to being a chainsaw, and the bike went back to being a regular bike.
